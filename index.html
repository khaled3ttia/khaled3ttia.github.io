Github Linkedin Resumé

I'm a PhD candidate at the University of Oklahoma, School of Computer Science. I received a MS degree in Computer Engineering from Lehigh University in 2020. Before that, I received a MS in Automatic Conrtol Engineering from Mansoura University, Egypt in 2017. I got my Bachelor in Computers and Control Systems Engineering from Mansoura University, Egypt in 2013.

My main research interests are: High-Performance Computing, Domain-specific computing, and Computer Architecture.

I like working on projects that make use of different high-performance tricks such as: parallelization, vectorization, reducing memory traffic, exploiting data locality, etc.

For that, I mainly use C/C++ in my projects, I ocassionaly use Python and Bash as well for automating experiments and plotting results.

I am interested in developing high-performance applications for heterogeneous architectures (CPU + GPU + distributed).

I am particularly interested in optimizing sparse matrix computations from the system point of view.

I develop using: CUDA, HIP, OpenMP, SYCL DPC++ and I make use of other helper tools such as clang sanitizers, heaptrack, and profilers such as perf, nvprof, and rocprof.
